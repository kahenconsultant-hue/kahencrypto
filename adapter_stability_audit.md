## Methodology

- `scope = scheduler`
- `event_type = scheduler_run_completed`
- `payload.executionEnvironment = production`

Collector health was then cross-checked against:

- `ingestion_logs`
- `raw_metrics`
- `raw_events`
- `source_health`

Important interpretation notes:

- Binance and Bybit are direct public REST collectors.
- CoinGecko and DefiLlama are emitted through the internal public adapter bundle, so their stability was inferred from metric provenance in `raw_metrics`.
- ETF has a primary source and a fallback path. Farside is blocked by Cloudflare, but The Block fallback is producing real ETF data.
- RSS is a group of RSS collectors, not a single endpoint. The report shows both run-level health and source-execution volume.
- No missing values were treated as zero.

## Cross-Adapter Observations

1. Exchange direct collectors are the main reliability weakness.
2. The internal public adapter bundle is succeeding, but it hides per-source detail for CoinGecko and DefiLlama.
3. ETF data is real, but primary source reliability is poor because Farside is Cloudflare-blocked.
4. FRED is usable but slow enough to matter for serverless execution budgets.
5. RSS is operationally healthy.
6. Stablecoin metrics are available, but their daily/delayed cadence must not be treated like 30-minute market data.

## Top 10 Remaining Operational Risks Before Phase 15.3

1. Binance direct REST failed in 48/48 evaluated production runs.
2. Bybit direct REST failed in 48/48 evaluated production runs.
3. Funding and open-interest reliability remains weak because both direct exchange collectors fail together.
4. ETF primary source Farside is Cloudflare-blocked in every run, making The Block fallback a single operational dependency.
5. DefiLlama stablecoin output is parsed correctly but only emitted once in the 48-run window; source cadence needs explicit daily/delayed semantics.
6. CoinGecko output parses cleanly but was only observed in 21/48 production runs; bundle-level success can hide per-source emission gaps.
7. FRED average response time is high at roughly 9.2 seconds, which can affect serverless cron reliability.
8. FRED partial macro degradation still occurs, especially around FEDFUNDS handling.
9. Scheduler production status is still mostly `success_with_limited_confidence`, not clean success, because market/ETF/fusion stages are degraded-but-operational.
10. Manual production runs are mixed into the last-48-run window. Long-term readiness should separate `external_cron_job_org` from `manual_http` when calculating production reliability.

## Recommended Next Actions

1. Fix Binance and Bybit direct endpoint/parsing behavior before treating derivatives reliability as stable.
2. Add explicit per-subadapter telemetry for CoinGecko and DefiLlama inside the internal public adapter bundle.
3. Keep ETF marked Degraded while Farside is blocked, even when The Block fallback succeeds.
4. Treat DefiLlama stablecoin data as daily/delayed, not 30-minute stale.
5. Separate external cron reliability from manual production runs in readiness calculations.
