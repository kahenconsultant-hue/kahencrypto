# Decision Model

Canonical intelligence postures:

- `increase_selective_risk`
- `maintain_risk`
- `reduce_risk`
- `defensive`
- `abstain`

`abstain` is a valid published decision posture. It means the system cannot issue a sufficiently supported directional posture. It does not mean the market is bearish and must not be mapped to `defensive`, `reduce_risk`, or any other directional posture.

Every decision must include:

- `posture`
- `plain_language`
- `model_action`
- `why`
- `why_not`
- `main_drivers`
- `contradictory_evidence`
- `largest_positive_driver`
- `largest_negative_driver`
- `unresolved_conflicts`
- `what_changed_since_previous`
- `historical_confirmation`
- `alternative_view`
- `invalidation_conditions`
- `monitor_next`

Decision synthesis must consider evidence quality, independence, hypothesis strength, contradiction burden, historical support, data completeness, regime stability, and trigger proximity.

Rules:

- No final posture may be produced solely from a composite score.
- A posture change requires evidence change.
- `abstain` is required when evidence is materially insufficient or conflicts are unresolved beyond acceptable thresholds.
- `abstain` must include structured abstention reasons, blocking conditions, required evidence to resume, and previous valid report policy.
- `abstain` must be reversible when the listed required evidence becomes available.
- A schema-invalid output is not an abstention report; it is invalid model output and must not be published.
- The decision must state why the selected posture prevailed over the alternative view.
- The decision must be phrased as a model posture, not personal advice.
