# Decision Philosophy

CMIP is a structured daily decision-support system. It is not a price-prediction system, a signal seller, or an automated portfolio manager.

The engine answers:

1. What is happening?
2. Why is it happening?
3. Why does it matter?
4. What changed?
5. What evidence supports the current view?
6. What evidence contradicts it?
7. What historical evidence confirms or weakens it?
8. What would invalidate the view?
9. What should be monitored next?
10. What posture would the model take today, and why?

## 6.1 No Single Indicator Decides The Market View

No individual signal may independently determine the final posture.

Examples:

- ETF inflows alone do not prove a bull regime.
- Low funding alone does not prove a safe market.
- Positive price momentum alone does not prove healthy liquidity.
- Historical similarity alone does not predict repetition.

A decision requires multi-domain confirmation.

## 6.2 Confirmation Must Come From Independent Domains

Evidence is stronger when it comes from independent mechanisms, such as ETF inflow, stablecoin expansion, improving breadth, supportive macro, and healthy derivatives. Repeated readings from one domain must not be counted as independent confirmation.

## 6.3 Contradictory Evidence Must Remain Visible

The engine must never hide evidence that opposes its preferred hypothesis.

Every final decision must include:

- strongest supporting evidence
- strongest contradictory evidence
- unresolved conflict
- reason the final posture still prevailed

## 6.4 Historical Evidence Is Contextual, Not Predictive

Historical evidence may strengthen or weaken a hypothesis, expose missing confirmation, and identify structural differences. It must never be presented as proof that the same outcome will happen again.

## 6.5 The Engine Must Be Allowed To Abstain

The system must explicitly support insufficient data, unresolved conflict, unstable regime, low confidence, and no-action posture. It must prefer "insufficient evidence" over fabricated certainty.

## 6.6 Decision Change Requires Evidence Change

The posture must not change merely because language generation changed. A posture change requires new evidence, changed evidence strength, changed historical confirmation, changed risk trigger, or changed data quality.

## 6.7 Simplicity For The User, Rigor Underneath

The final explanation must be understandable to a non-professional user. Simple language must preserve uncertainty, contradiction, limitations, evidence traceability, and invalidation conditions.

## 6.8 No Hidden Recommendation

The system expresses model posture, model action, and hypothetical committee view. It must not express personalized advice.

Preferred phrasing:

> اگر این مدل با داده‌های فعلی تصمیم می‌گرفت، موضع آن حفظ ریسک بود.

Forbidden phrasing:

> شما باید بخرید.

## 6.9 Every Conclusion Must Be Falsifiable

Every important conclusion must include what would prove it wrong, what threshold changes the posture, and what missing evidence is still required.

## 6.10 Accountability Over Persuasion

The system prioritizes traceability, honesty, reproducibility, decision memory, and error review over sounding confident or persuasive.
