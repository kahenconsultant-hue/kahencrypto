# Evidence Quality Model

Evidence quality evaluates whether an observation is safe to use. It is not the same as importance.

Quality dimensions:

- source tier
- freshness
- completeness
- agreement across sources
- method transparency
- direct versus proxy status
- calculation trace
- identity certainty
- sample quality
- survivorship bias risk
- publication delay
- revision risk

Rules:

1. Evidence importance is not evidence quality.
2. High-importance evidence with low quality must not dominate a decision.
3. Multiple low-quality sources do not equal one verified source.
4. Repeated syndication of one news article is not independent confirmation.
5. Proxy evidence requires method disclosure and lower reliability unless independently verified.
6. Delayed official releases must preserve observation date and publication delay.
7. Revised macro or economic data must carry revision risk.
8. Historical evidence quality depends on event definition, sample selection, sample size, regime compatibility, and limitations.

Quality effects:

- Low quality can reduce strength.
- Low quality can cap confidence.
- Low quality can force abstention when the affected field is critical.
- Low quality cannot be hidden by polished explanation language.
